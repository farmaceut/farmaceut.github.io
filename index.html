Research Areas

Senotherapeutic Agents

Anticancer Drugs

Senotherapeutic agents target senescent cells or their inducing factors, aiming to prevent age-related diseases and potentially extend healthspan. Computer-aided drug design in this field holds great promise, facilitating the generation of multiple hits before wet lab studies, thereby accelerating research progress.
A variety of agents with diverse mechanisms of action, including photosensitizers, platinum compounds, and BODIPY agents, are deployed against cancer cells. Given the emergence of resistance in certain neoplastic cells to existing substances, computational chemistry plays a pivotal role in advancing these treatments by predicting efficacy and selective toxicity of new drugs.

Dietary Antioxidants

Catalysis

Negative oxidative stress, stemming from an inadequate supply of biological antioxidants, leads to the oxidation of biomolecules and subsequent cellular dysfunction. Polyphenols, widely acknowledged for their significant antioxidative activity, exert both direct and indirect effects against radicals.Understanding the mechanism underlying this activity could rationalize the intake of polyphenols, potentially mitigating oxidative stress-related cellular dysfunction.
Catalysis, a fundamental branch of chemistry, accelerates chemical reactions through catalysts. Computational chemistry plays a key role by elucidating reaction mechanisms, designing innovative catalysts, and optimizing catalytic processes to achieve heightened efficiency and selectivity.
